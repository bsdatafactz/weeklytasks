CRITICAL: If you suspect an active security breach or ransomware attack, immediately call the Security Incident Hotline: 1-800-555-0911 (24/7). Do not attempt to remediate on your own.

1. Purpose & Scope

This policy establishes Contoso Corp's framework for detecting, responding to, and recovering from information security incidents. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who access Contoso Corp systems, and to all systems, applications, and data owned or managed by Contoso Corp regardless of hosting location.

3. Incident Response Phases

Phase 1: Identification & Reporting

Any employee who suspects a security incident must report it immediately via:

  • Security Incident Hotline: 1-800-555-0911 (P1/P2 — 24/7)
  • IT Help Desk: ext. 4357 or helpdesk@contosocorp.com (P3/P4)
  • Email: security@contosocorp.com (non-urgent reports)
  • Outlook Report Phishing button (for suspicious emails)

When reporting, provide: your name and contact info, systems or data affected, how you discovered the issue, and any actions already taken (including screenshots if safe to capture).

Phase 2: Containment

The Security Operations Center (SOC) will direct containment actions. Employees must follow SOC instructions immediately and completely. Common containment actions include:

  • Isolating affected systems from the network (do not power off unless instructed).
  • Resetting compromised credentials.
  • Revoking access tokens or API keys.
  • Blocking malicious IP addresses or domains at the perimeter.

Phase 3: Eradication & Recovery

The SOC and IT Operations teams will remove the threat, restore from clean backups, patch vulnerabilities, and validate system integrity before returning systems to production. No affected system may return to production without CISO sign-off for P1/P2 incidents.

Phase 4: Post-Incident Review

Within 5 business days of incident resolution, the SOC will produce an Incident Report covering: timeline, root cause, impact assessment, containment and remediation actions, and recommended preventive measures. P1/P2 reports are shared with the CEO, CFO, and Board Audit Committee. All incidents are logged in the Security Incident Registry.

5. Evidence Preservation

Employees must not delete, modify, or overwrite any data or logs related to a suspected incident. All evidence must be preserved in its original state. The SOC may implement a legal hold on affected systems. Failure to preserve evidence may constitute obstruction and result in disciplinary action.

6. Communication During an Incident

All external communications about security incidents (to media, regulators, clients, or partners) must be approved by the CISO, Legal, and Communications team. Employees must not discuss incidents on social media, in public forums, or with unauthorized parties. Internal communications should use the designated incident channel in Microsoft Teams, not standard email, during an active incident.
