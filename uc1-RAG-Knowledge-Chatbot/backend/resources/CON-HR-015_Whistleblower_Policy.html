1. Purpose & Legal Basis

This policy establishes Contoso Corp's commitment to ethical conduct and the mechanisms by which employees and contractors may safely report concerns about illegal activity, financial misconduct, safety violations, or serious breaches of company policy. It fulfills obligations under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX Section 806), the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform Act, OSHA whistleblower protection programs, and equivalent laws in all jurisdictions where Contoso Corp operates.

Contoso Corp's Audit Committee of the Board of Directors has oversight responsibility for this policy and receives quarterly reports on whistleblower activity (de-identified and aggregated).

2. What to Report

Employees are encouraged — and in some cases legally obligated — to report any reasonable, good-faith concern regarding:

  • Financial Fraud & Accounting: Falsification of financial records, fraudulent expense reports, improper revenue recognition, off-balance-sheet transactions, or material misstatements in public filings.
  • Securities Law Violations: Insider trading, market manipulation, or failure to disclose material information.
  • Bribery & Corruption: Any offer, payment, or receipt of improper benefits to influence business or government decisions.
  • Data Privacy & Security: Unauthorized disclosure of personal or confidential data; concealment of a data breach.
  • Workplace Safety: Concealment of safety incidents, falsification of safety records, or conditions posing imminent risk of serious harm.
  • Conflicts of Interest: Undisclosed relationships that influence business decisions for personal gain.
  • Discrimination & Harassment: Systemic conduct or conduct by senior leaders that has not been addressed through normal HR channels.
  • Violations of Company Policy: Serious or repeated violations of Contoso Corp's Code of Conduct or other policies.
Note: This policy is not a substitute for normal HR processes for routine workplace disputes, performance concerns, or general dissatisfaction. For those matters, contact your HR Business Partner directly.

4. Anonymity & Confidentiality

You may report concerns anonymously through the Ethics Hotline or Web Portal. Contoso Corp will protect the confidentiality of your identity to the extent possible under law. There are two situations where complete anonymity cannot be guaranteed:

  • If disclosure is required by law or court order.
  • If you have provided information that would make your identity apparent regardless of our efforts (e.g., you are the only person with access to specific records you describe).

Anonymous reporters are encouraged to provide enough detail to enable a meaningful investigation. Vague or unsubstantiated anonymous reports may be difficult to investigate fully.

6. Non-Retaliation Commitment

Contoso Corp strictly prohibits any form of retaliation against any person who: (a) reports a concern in good faith under this policy; (b) participates in an investigation; (c) refuses to participate in activity they reasonably believe is illegal or violates this policy; or (d) exercises any right protected by whistleblower laws.

This protection applies even if the reported concern is investigated and found to be unsubstantiated, provided the report was made in good faith and not with the intent to harm.

7. What Constitutes Retaliation

Retaliation includes any adverse action taken — or threatened — because of a protected report. Examples include:

  • Termination, demotion, reduction in hours or pay, or denial of promotion
  • Negative performance reviews or disciplinary actions timed to coincide with a report
  • Hostile treatment, exclusion, or social isolation by colleagues or management
  • Reassignment to less desirable duties, shifts, or locations
  • Threats, intimidation, or harassment of any kind
  • Disclosing the reporter's identity without consent

If you believe you are experiencing retaliation, report it immediately to the Ethics Hotline, HR, or Legal. Retaliating against a whistleblower is itself a serious policy violation and may violate federal and state law, resulting in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

9. False Reports

While Contoso Corp strongly encourages reporting and protects good-faith reporters, knowingly making a false report with the intent to harm another person or the company is a serious violation of the Code of Conduct (CON-HR-001) and may result in disciplinary action up to termination, as well as potential legal liability. The good-faith standard does not require that a report be correct — only that the reporter genuinely believed the concern to be true at the time of filing.

10. Manager & HR Obligations

  • Managers who receive a whistleblower complaint — whether verbally or in writing — must escalate to Legal or HR within 24 hours. Managers may not investigate such reports independently.
  • Managers must not discourage employees from making reports, suggest reports are futile, or imply negative consequences for reporting.
  • HR is responsible for monitoring the work environment of known reporters for signs of retaliation for 12 months post-report.
  • All persons involved in handling whistleblower reports are bound by strict confidentiality obligations.
