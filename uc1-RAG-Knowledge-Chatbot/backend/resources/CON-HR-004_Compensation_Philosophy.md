## 1. Philosophy & Guiding Principles

Contoso Corp's compensation philosophy is designed to attract, retain, and motivate top talent by offering pay that is:

- **Market-competitive:** We target the 50th–65th percentile of the relevant labor market for base pay. High-performing employees and critical roles may be positioned above the 65th percentile.
- **Performance-differentiated:** Pay differentiation reflects individual and team performance, not tenure alone.
- **Equitable:** We conduct annual pay equity analyses and address unexplained disparities by protected class.
- **Transparent:** Pay ranges for each grade level are published on the HR intranet. Employees are encouraged to have open conversations about compensation with their HR Business Partner.
- **Compliant:** All compensation practices comply with the Fair Labor Standards Act, state wage laws, and applicable international labor standards.

## 2. Job Architecture

Contoso Corp uses a structured job architecture with 13 grade levels:

| Grade Level | Typical Titles | Pay Range (Base, USD) |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Grade 3 | Associate / Coordinator | $45,000 – $65,000 |
| Grade 4 | Specialist / Analyst | $60,000 – $80,000 |
| Grade 5 | Senior Specialist / Senior Analyst | $75,000 – $100,000 |
| Grade 6 | Lead / Staff | $90,000 – $120,000 |
| Grade 7 | Principal / Senior Lead | $110,000 – $145,000 |
| Grade 8 | Manager | $130,000 – $170,000 |
| Grade 9 | Senior Manager | $155,000 – $200,000 |
| Grade 10 | Director | $185,000 – $250,000 |
| Grade 11 | Senior Director | $220,000 – $300,000 |
| Grade 12 | VP | $275,000 – $380,000 |
| Grade 13 | SVP / Executive Director | $350,000 – $500,000 |
| Grade 14 | EVP / C-1 Officer | $450,000+ |
| Grade 15 | C-Suite (CEO, CFO, CHRO, etc.) | Per Board compensation committee |

> **Note:** Ranges are reviewed annually and adjusted based on market data (Willis Towers Watson, Radford, and Mercer surveys). Individual pay within the range reflects experience, performance, and geographic differential.

## 3. Geographic Pay Differentials

Contoso Corp applies geographic differentials to base pay to reflect cost-of-labor differences across markets. The pay tables above reflect the **national median** reference point. Actual offers and pay adjustments are indexed as follows:

| Market Zone | Differential vs. National Median | Example Locations |
|------------|----------------------------------|-------------------|
| Zone 1 — High | +25% to +40% | San Francisco Bay Area, New York City, Seattle |
| Zone 2 — Above Average | +10% to +24% | Boston, Los Angeles, Washington DC, Austin |
| Zone 3 — Standard | 0% to +9% | Chicago, Denver, Atlanta, Phoenix |
| Zone 4 — Below Average | -5% to 0% | Secondary markets, Midwest, Mid-South |

Remote employees hired into specific roles are paid based on their home state/metro, not the office location. Geographic differential changes require VP and HR approval.

## 4. Annual Merit Process

Merit increases are awarded annually effective March 1, based on the prior-year performance rating:

| Performance Rating | Typical Merit Budget Range |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| 5 — Exceptional | 6% – 9% |
| 4 — Exceeds Expectations | 4% – 6% |
| 3 — Meets Expectations | 2% – 4% |
| 2 — Needs Improvement | 0% – 1% |
| 1 — Unsatisfactory | 0% |

Merit increases are constrained by the grade range maximum. Employees already at or above the range maximum may receive a one-time lump-sum bonus in lieu of a base increase.

> **Note:** Merit increases are not guaranteed. They are subject to company financial performance and board approval of the annual compensation budget.

## 5. Variable Compensation (Bonus)

### 5.1 Annual Incentive Plan (AIP)

Eligible employees (Grade 5 and above) participate in the Annual Incentive Plan. Target bonus percentages by grade:

| Grade | Target Bonus (% of Base) |
|-------|--------------------------|
| 5–6 | 10% |
| 7–8 | 15% |
| 9–10 | 20% |
| 11–12 | 30% |
| 13–14 | 40% |
| 15 (C-Suite) | 50% – 100% |

Actual bonus payout = Target Bonus × Company Performance Multiplier × Individual Performance Multiplier. The Company Performance Multiplier (0–2.0x) is determined by the Board based on achievement of annual revenue, EBITDA, and strategic objectives.

### 5.2 Spot Bonuses

Managers may award spot bonuses of $500–$2,500 (non-exempt) or $500–$5,000 (exempt) to recognize exceptional one-time contributions. Spot bonuses require VP approval and are processed through Workday. They are taxable income and subject to applicable withholding.

---

## 6. Equity Compensation

Equity grants (Restricted Stock Units / RSUs) are available to Grade 8 and above:

- **New hire grants** are made at time of offer and approved by the Compensation Committee.
- **Vesting schedule:** 4-year cliff/graded vest (25% at 1-year cliff, then monthly vesting for 36 months).
- **Annual refresh grants** are awarded to Grade 9+ based on performance, typically in Q1.
- Equity plan details and grant agreements are managed through Carta (carta.com).

---

## 7. Pay Equity Commitment

Contoso Corp conducts an annual pay equity analysis with external counsel. The analysis controls for legitimate, job-related factors (grade, tenure, performance, geography) and identifies unexplained pay gaps by gender, race/ethnicity, and other protected characteristics. Remediation budgets are allocated as needed. Results are reviewed by the Board Compensation Committee and a summary is published in our annual Corporate Responsibility Report.

---

## 8. Compensation Discussions

Employees have the legal right to discuss their pay with colleagues. Contoso Corp actively supports pay transparency. Managers are not permitted to discourage compensation discussions. However, sharing confidential compensation information of other employees (e.g., individual peer salaries obtained through system access) without their consent is a privacy violation.

---

## 9. Off-Cycle Adjustments

Off-cycle (mid-year) compensation adjustments are limited to:

- Promotions (effective on promotion date)
- Retention awards approved by VP and CHRO
- Market correction adjustments identified through formal pay equity review
- Corrections of administrative errors

All off-cycle adjustments require VP approval, HR Business Partner support, and Compensation team review.
