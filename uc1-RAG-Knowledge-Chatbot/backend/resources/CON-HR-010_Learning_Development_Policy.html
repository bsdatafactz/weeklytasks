1. Philosophy

Contoso Corp believes that learning is a continuous, lifelong process and one of the most important investments we make in our people. We are committed to providing every employee with access to resources, programs, and opportunities that enable them to grow professionally and personally, advance their careers, and adapt to a rapidly changing technology landscape.

2. Learning Programs Overview

Tuition Reimbursement Program

Annual Benefit
$5,250/year

Available for accredited undergraduate and graduate courses (degree or certificate programs) directly related to your current role or a clearly defined career path at Contoso Corp. Pre-approval from manager and HR is required before enrollment. Minimum grade requirement: B (3.0/4.0). Employees must remain with the company for 12 months post-reimbursement or repay on a pro-rated basis. Tax-free up to IRS annual limit.

Professional Certification Sponsorship

Per Certification
Up to $3,000

Contoso Corp sponsors professional certifications aligned to company technology stack and job families. Covers exam fees, prep materials, and one re-take. Priority certifications include: Microsoft Azure (AZ-900 through AZ-305), AWS, PMP, CISSP, CPA, CFA, and Scrum/Agile certifications. Submit requests via the L&D portal in Workday. No repayment obligation if certification is achieved.

Online Learning Platforms

Unlimited Access
No Cost to Employee

All employees have unlimited access to LinkedIn Learning, Pluralsight (for technical roles), and Harvard ManageMentor (for managers Grade 8+). Access is provisioned automatically during onboarding. Employees should dedicate at least 20 hours per year to structured online learning; this is tracked in Workday and factored into development discussions.

Conference & External Training

Annual Budget
$2,000/year

Employees may request budget for external conferences, workshops, bootcamps, or instructor-led training. Requests require manager and VP approval for amounts over $1,000. Employees attending conferences on company time are expected to share key learnings with their team within 30 days (lunch-and-learn, blog post, or team presentation).

4. Mentorship & Coaching

Contoso Corp runs a formal mentorship program matching employees with senior mentors from across the business. Employees may request a mentor once per year through the L&D portal. Mentors commit to monthly 1-hour sessions for a 6-month engagement. Separately, executive coaching is available to Grade 10+ employees through our external coaching pool — contact the CHRO's office to request.

5. Individual Development Plans (IDPs)

All employees are expected to maintain an active Individual Development Plan (IDP) in Workday. IDPs should include:

  • 1–3 development goals for the current year, aligned to career aspirations.
  • Specific learning actions (courses, projects, coaching) with target completion dates.
  • Skills to develop or deepen, mapped to Contoso Corp's competency framework.
  • Progress reviewed quarterly during one-on-one meetings with the manager.

IDPs are the employee's document and are not part of the formal performance evaluation. They are a voluntary tool for growth planning.

7. Eligibility & Conditions

  • Employees on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) are not eligible for tuition reimbursement or conference budget during the PIP period.
  • Employees must be in good standing (no active disciplinary action) to participate in selective internal programs.
  • Learning activities during work hours require manager approval if they exceed 4 hours per week.
  • Part-time employees are eligible for all programs on a pro-rated basis.
