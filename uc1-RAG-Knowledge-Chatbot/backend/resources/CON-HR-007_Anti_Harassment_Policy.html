1. Purpose & Commitment

Contoso Corp is unconditionally committed to providing a workplace free from harassment, discrimination, and retaliation for all employees, applicants, contractors, clients, and visitors. This policy reflects our core value of Respect and our legal obligations under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act, the Americans with Disabilities Act, the Age Discrimination in Employment Act, and equivalent laws in all jurisdictions where we operate.

Contoso Corp will not tolerate any form of harassment or discrimination, regardless of the seniority of the perpetrator. Senior leaders are held to a higher standard by virtue of their positions of authority.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all work-related settings including:

  • Company offices, facilities, and remote work environments
  • Client sites and partner locations when representing Contoso Corp
  • Company-sponsored events, travel, conferences, and social gatherings
  • Digital communications including email, Teams, Slack, and social media when related to work

4. Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct is prohibited and may constitute harassment:

  • Verbal: Derogatory comments, slurs, jokes, or nicknames based on a protected characteristic; unwelcome comments about someone's appearance or body.
  • Physical: Unwelcome touching, blocking movement, gestures of an offensive or threatening nature.
  • Visual/Digital: Displaying or distributing offensive images, memes, GIFs, or symbols; sending inappropriate text, email, or messages.
  • Workplace Exclusion: Deliberate isolation, exclusion from meetings or social events based on a protected characteristic.
  • Microaggressions: While a single ambiguous incident may not rise to the level of harassment, patterns of conduct that communicate hostility or inferiority toward a protected group are taken seriously.

5. Sexual Harassment Defined

Sexual harassment is a form of sex discrimination and includes:

5.1 Quid Pro Quo Harassment

Conditioning employment benefits (promotion, continued employment, assignments) on the acceptance of unwelcome sexual conduct. Even a single incident can constitute quid pro quo harassment.

5.2 Hostile Work Environment

Unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature that is sufficiently severe or pervasive to alter the conditions of employment and create an abusive working environment. Examples include: sexual comments or jokes, unwanted romantic advances, displaying sexually explicit material, or persistent requests for dates after a clear refusal.

Important: The standard is the perspective of a reasonable person in the complainant's position. Intent is not a defense. "I was just joking" does not make conduct acceptable if it created a hostile environment.

7. Investigation Process

All complaints will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and impartially:

  • HR will acknowledge receipt within 1 business day.
  • An investigation will commence within 5 business days of a formal complaint.
  • Investigations are conducted by HR or, for senior personnel, by external counsel.
  • Both the complainant and respondent will have the opportunity to present their account and evidence.
  • Findings will be documented and communicated to both parties within 30 business days where feasible.
  • Confidentiality will be maintained to the extent possible; complete confidentiality cannot always be guaranteed.

8. Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against any person who reports a concern, participates in an investigation, or exercises rights under this policy is strictly prohibited and is a separate, serious violation. Retaliation includes adverse employment actions (demotion, termination, reduced hours), hostile treatment, exclusion, or other conduct that would deter a reasonable person from reporting. Retaliation may result in disciplinary action independent of the outcome of the underlying complaint.

9. Consequences

Confirmed violations of this policy will result in disciplinary action proportionate to the severity and frequency of the conduct, up to and including immediate termination of employment. Certain conduct may also be referred to law enforcement authorities. Disciplinary action will be administered consistently regardless of the seniority or tenure of the violator.

10. Training Requirements

All employees must complete the Preventing Workplace Harassment training module in Workday annually. New hires must complete the training within 30 days of hire. Managers must complete an enhanced version covering investigation responsibilities. Training completion is tracked and required for year-end performance completion.
