1. Purpose

This policy establishes the standards and procedures for purchasing goods and services at Contoso Corp. It ensures prudent stewardship of company funds, consistent vendor evaluation, compliance with anti-corruption laws, and appropriate protection of Contoso Corp's data and intellectual property in vendor relationships.

3. Approved Vendor List

Contoso Corp maintains an Approved Vendor List (AVL) for frequently used categories. All purchases exceeding $5,000 must use an AVL vendor or go through new vendor onboarding. The AVL is maintained by the Procurement team and published on the Finance intranet. New vendors must complete:

  • Vendor Registration Form (Form FIN-V01)
  • W-9 (domestic) or W-8 (international)
  • Third-Party Risk Assessment (security questionnaire for technology vendors)
  • Anti-corruption certification
  • Insurance certificate (minimum requirements per vendor category)

5. Contract Management

All contracts must be reviewed by the Legal Department before signature. No employee may bind Contoso Corp contractually except as authorized by the Delegation of Authority Policy (CON-FIN-003). Contracts must be executed through the CLM system (Ironclad) and stored in the Contract Repository. Key requirements:

  • All contracts must include: governing law clause (Delaware, unless otherwise agreed), limitation of liability, IP ownership, confidentiality, and termination rights.
  • Software and technology contracts must include data processing addenda addressing security standards, breach notification, and data return/deletion.
  • Automatic renewal clauses must be flagged; Procurement must be notified 90 days before automatic renewal of contracts over $25,000.

6. Vendor Performance Management

Vendors with annual spend over $100,000 are subject to formal quarterly performance reviews against defined KPIs. Procurement maintains vendor scorecards in Coupa. Vendors with consecutive poor performance ratings may be placed on a performance improvement plan or removed from the AVL. All vendor issues must be documented in Coupa, not handled informally.

7. Conflicts of Interest in Procurement

Employees involved in procurement decisions must disclose any personal or financial relationship with potential vendors to their manager and the Ethics team. Employees may not participate in vendor selection if they have a financial interest in the vendor or a close personal relationship with vendor personnel. Undisclosed conflicts are a violation of the Code of Conduct (CON-HR-001) and may result in termination.
