1. Purpose & Commitment

Contoso Corp is committed to providing reasonable accommodations to qualified employees and applicants with disabilities, and to employees with sincerely held religious beliefs, to enable them to perform the essential functions of their role and fully participate in the workplace. This policy reflects our legal obligations under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), the Pregnancy Workers Fairness Act (PWFA), and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act.

Core Principle: Accommodation is a collaborative, individualized process — not a one-size-fits-all benefit. Contoso Corp engages in an Interactive Process with each employee to identify the most effective accommodation that does not create an undue hardship.

2. Types of Accommodations

2.1 Disability Accommodations (ADA)

A disability under the ADA is a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities. Examples of accommodations Contoso Corp has provided include:

  • Modified work schedules or shift changes
  • Ergonomic equipment (chairs, desks, keyboards, monitors)
  • Screen reader or other assistive technology software
  • Dedicated quiet workspace for employees with sensory sensitivities
  • Remote work arrangements (in coordination with CON-HR-009)
  • Additional break time for medical needs
  • Temporary reassignment to an equivalent available position
  • Leave beyond FMLA entitlement as a reasonable accommodation

2.2 Religious Accommodations (Title VII)

Contoso Corp accommodates employees' sincerely held religious beliefs, practices, and observances unless doing so would cause more than a de minimis hardship. Examples include schedule adjustments for religious holidays or observances, time and space for prayer, and dress code modifications for religious dress.

2.3 Pregnancy Accommodations (PWFA)

Under the Pregnancy Workers Fairness Act (effective June 2023), employees with known limitations related to pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions are entitled to reasonable accommodations. Employees need not show that their limitation rises to the level of a disability. Accommodations may include: more frequent rest breaks, modified duties, temporary reassignment, telework, or a modified schedule.

3. Interactive Process — Step by Step

1

Employee Request

Employee submits an Accommodation Request Form (available on the HR intranet) or contacts HR at accommodations@contosocorp.com. A verbal request is also sufficient to trigger the process.

2

HR Acknowledgment

HR acknowledges receipt within 2 business days and assigns an HR Business Partner as the primary point of contact for the process.

3

Medical Documentation (if needed)

HR may request documentation from a licensed healthcare provider confirming the disability or limitation, its functional restrictions, and the recommended accommodation. Documentation is submitted directly to HR — not the manager — and kept strictly confidential.

4

Interactive Discussion

HR meets with the employee (and where necessary, the manager) to discuss the functional limitations, available accommodations, and the employee's preferences. The goal is a collaborative solution.

5

Decision & Implementation

HR documents the agreed accommodation in a written Accommodation Agreement. The accommodation is implemented within a reasonable timeframe (typically 5–10 business days; urgency is considered for medical necessity).

6

Review

HR checks in with the employee at 30 days and 90 days after implementation. Accommodations may be modified if circumstances change.

4. Confidentiality

All accommodation requests and related medical information are strictly confidential. This information is maintained separately from the personnel file, accessible only to HR and, on a need-to-know basis, to those directly involved in implementing the accommodation. Managers will be informed only of the accommodation needed and any work restrictions — not the underlying diagnosis or medical details.

5. Anti-Retaliation

Employees who request or receive accommodations, or who participate in the Interactive Process on behalf of another employee, are protected from retaliation. Adverse actions against an employee for requesting an accommodation are a violation of this policy and applicable law. Report any suspected retaliation to HR or the Ethics Hotline (1-800-555-0199).
