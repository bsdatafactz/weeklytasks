# CON-FIN-003 — Delegation of Authority & Signing Authority Policy

**Company:** Contoso Corp  
**Division:** Finance & Legal  
**Policy ID:** CON-FIN-003  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Reviewed:** December 2023  
**Policy Owner:** Chief Financial Officer  
**Applies To:** All Employees with Approval or Signature Responsibilities  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines who at Contoso Corp has authority to approve expenditures, enter into contracts, and execute financial commitments on behalf of the company. Unauthorized commitments — even verbal ones — can create binding legal obligations for Contoso Corp. No employee may commit company resources beyond their delegated authority limit.

---

## 2. General Principles

- **Authority is role-based, not person-based.** Authority is granted to the holder of a position, not to a named individual. Acting officers assume the authority of the role they are temporarily filling.
- **Authority cannot be re-delegated downward** beyond one level without CFO written approval.
- **All approvals must be documented** in the relevant system of record (Workday, Concur, Coupa, or CLM) before the commitment is made.
- **Combined transaction splitting is prohibited.** Breaking a single purchase or contract into smaller pieces to stay within a lower approval tier is a policy violation subject to disciplinary action.
- **Urgent approvals:** If the appropriate approver is unavailable for a time-sensitive matter, escalate to the next level up — do not proceed without approval.

## 3. Financial Expenditure Authority

### 3.1 Operating Expenses (OpEx)

| Role / Grade | Single Transaction Limit | Annual Budget Authority |
|-------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Employee (all grades) | Not applicable (submit requests) | N/A |
| Manager (Grade 8) | $5,000 | $50,000 |
| Senior Manager (Grade 9) | $15,000 | $150,000 |
| Director (Grade 10) | $50,000 | $500,000 |
| Senior Director (Grade 11) | $100,000 | $1,000,000 |
| VP (Grade 12) | $250,000 | $2,500,000 |
| SVP / EVP (Grade 13–14) | $500,000 | $5,000,000 |
| CFO | $1,000,000 | Unlimited (within Board-approved budget) |
| CEO | $2,000,000 | Unlimited (within Board-approved budget) |
| Board of Directors | Unlimited | Unlimited |

### 3.2 Capital Expenditures (CapEx)

Capital expenditure thresholds are 50% of the OpEx limits above for the same role. All CapEx over $250,000 requires Finance Committee review. All CapEx over $1,000,000 requires Board approval regardless of role.

### 3.3 Unbudgeted Expenditures

Any expenditure not included in the current approved annual budget requires approval one level above the standard threshold:

- Unbudgeted OpEx up to $50,000 → Director approval
- Unbudgeted OpEx $50,001–$250,000 → VP + CFO approval
- Unbudgeted OpEx over $250,000 → CFO + CEO approval
- Unbudgeted CapEx → CFO + CEO approval regardless of amount

## 4. Contract Signing Authority

### 4.1 Commercial Contracts (Customer, Vendor, Partner)

| Contract Value (Total Commitment) | Signing Authority |
|-----------------------------------|------------------|
| Under $25,000 | Director (with Legal review if non-standard terms) |
| $25,001 – $100,000 | VP + Legal sign-off |
| $100,001 – $500,000 | SVP + CFO + Legal |
| $500,001 – $2,000,000 | CEO + CFO + Legal |
| Over $2,000,000 | CEO + CFO + Legal + Board notification |
| Any contract with unlimited liability | CEO + General Counsel + Board approval |
| Any contract with IP assignment from Contoso Corp | CEO + General Counsel required |

> **Important:** "Total commitment" means the full contract value over the entire term, including optional renewal periods if exercisable by the other party.

### 4.2 Employment Agreements

| Agreement Type | Signing Authority |
|---------------|------------------|
| Standard offer letter (all grades) | HR Director or above |
| Offer letter with non-standard terms (sign-on bonus, relocation, enhanced severance) | CHRO + CFO |
| Executive offer letter (VP and above) | CEO + CHRO + Legal |
| Separation agreement (standard) | HR Director |
| Separation agreement with payment over $50,000 | CHRO + CFO + Legal |
| Non-compete or non-solicitation agreement | General Counsel |

### 4.3 Real Estate & Facilities

| Transaction | Signing Authority |
|------------|------------------|
| Office lease under $500,000/year | CFO + VP Facilities |
| Office lease $500,001 – $2,000,000/year | CFO + CEO |
| Office lease over $2,000,000/year | CFO + CEO + Board approval |
| Lease amendment or early termination | Same thresholds as original lease value |
| Facilities services contract | Per commercial contract table above |

## 5. Banking & Treasury Authority

| Transaction | Authorized By |
|------------|--------------|
| Open or close a bank account | CFO + CEO |
| Wire transfer under $100,000 | Controller or above (dual approval required) |
| Wire transfer $100,001 – $1,000,000 | CFO + Controller (dual approval) |
| Wire transfer over $1,000,000 | CFO + CEO (dual approval) |
| Establish line of credit or debt facility | CFO + CEO + Board |
| Investment of cash over $5,000,000 | CFO + Board Treasurer |

All wire transfers require dual authorization from two authorized signatories, regardless of amount. Single-authorizer wires are not permitted except in documented system emergencies approved by the CFO in advance.

---

## 6. Delegating Authority During Absence

When an authorized approver will be absent for more than 3 consecutive business days, they must:

1. Designate a temporary delegate of equal or higher authority in Workday.
2. Notify Finance and their own manager of the delegation period and scope.
3. The delegation expires automatically on the stated return date.

Temporary delegates may not further re-delegate. Unlimited or Board-level authority cannot be delegated informally; contact the CFO's office for coverage.

---

## 7. Compliance & Audit

The Internal Audit team performs quarterly reviews of transactions to verify compliance with this policy. Transactions found to exceed delegated authority limits will be reported to the CFO and relevant VP, and may be escalated to the Audit Committee. Employees who execute unauthorized commitments may be held personally liable for resulting costs and are subject to disciplinary action up to termination.

All delegation of authority exceptions must be submitted to the CFO's office in writing and logged in the DoA Exception Register maintained by Finance.
